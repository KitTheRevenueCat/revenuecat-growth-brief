# RevenueCat Growth Brief — Video Script

## Format
- 1–3 minute screen recording
- synthesized voiceover
- product demo + positioning

## Script

### Scene 1 — Hook
**Visual:** Homepage hero + brief panel

**Voiceover:**
RevenueCat's Charts API already gives teams access to revenue, MRR, churn, trial conversion, and customer growth. But most operators don't need more charts. They need faster answers to three questions: what changed, why it matters, and what to investigate next.

### Scene 2 — Product intro
**Visual:** KPI strip + investigation queue

**Voiceover:**
This is RevenueCat Growth Brief — a brief-first monetization operator built on top of the Charts API. Instead of rebuilding RevenueCat's dashboard, it wraps a thin command-center shell around a weekly investigation brief.

### Scene 3 — Brief value
**Visual:** Scroll through ranked brief findings

**Voiceover:**
The core idea is simple: compare recent performance against the previous window, detect meaningful changes, and produce a ranked operator brief. The output isn't just metrics. It's a decision-oriented queue: what needs attention first, what evidence supports it, and what to check next.

### Scene 4 — Supporting charts
**Visual:** Revenue / MRR / conversion / churn cards

**Voiceover:**
The charts are here as supporting evidence, not as the main product. Revenue, MRR, conversion, and churn give the operator just enough context to validate the brief without drowning in dashboard sprawl.

### Scene 5 — Product philosophy
**Visual:** README / architecture / code glimpse

**Voiceover:**
I intentionally kept this deterministic and high-trust. No fake AI causality, no forecasting theater, no giant BI clone. The product thesis is to build the insight layer on top of RevenueCat's existing charts.

### Scene 6 — CTA
**Visual:** Repo page

**Voiceover:**
If you're building a subscription app, this pattern is a useful starting point: keep the charts, but add an operator workflow on top. Clone the repo, connect your RevenueCat project, and turn chart data into a weekly growth brief your team can actually use.
